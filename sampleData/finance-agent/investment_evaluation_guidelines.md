# Investment Evaluation Guidelines for Energy Projects

## Capital Investment Criteria

### Hurdle Rate Determination

The minimum acceptable rate of return (hurdle rate) for energy projects should be calculated as follows:

1. **Base Components**
   - Risk-free rate (10-year Treasury yield)
   - Industry beta (typically 0.9-1.4 for integrated energy)
   - Equity risk premium (5-7% based on market conditions)
   - Size premium (0-3% based on project scale)
   - Country risk premium (0-10% based on location)

2. **Project-Specific Risk Adjustments**
   - Technology risk factor (0-5%)
   - Commodity price risk factor (1-4%)
   - Regulatory/policy risk factor (0-6%)
   - Execution risk factor (1-3%)

3. **Strategic Value Considerations**
   - Portfolio diversification benefit (-1% to 0%)
   - Operational synergy benefit (-2% to 0%)
   - Growth option value (-2% to 0%)
   - ESG impact adjustment (-1% to +3%)

## NPV and IRR Calculation Standards

### Cash Flow Modeling Requirements

1. **Revenue Projections**
   - Price deck: Use approved corporate price forecast with sensitivity cases (±15%, ±30%)
   - Production profiles: P50 case with P90/P10 sensitivities
   - Risk factors: Apply risk adjustment factors to both volume and price
   - Revenue timing: Model actual receipt timing, not production timing

2. **Cost Elements**
   - Capital expenditures: Include all direct and allocated costs with appropriate contingency (15-25%)
   - Operating expenses: Fixed vs. variable split with inflation factors
   - Overhead allocation: Corporate overhead at standard rates (3-7% of opex)
   - Abandonment costs: Full ARO accounting with appropriate discounting
   - Inflation: Differential inflation for various cost categories

3. **Fiscal Terms**
   - Royalties: Full modeling of sliding scales where applicable
   - Production taxes: Current rates with sensitivity for potential changes
   - Income taxes: Full provision including timing of deductions
   - Special considerations: PSCs, service contracts, tax incentives

4. **Financial Adjustments**
   - Working capital requirements: Typically 30-60 days of revenue
   - Financing effects: Leverage impact where appropriate
   - Currency effects: Local vs. reporting currency adjustments

### Evaluation Metrics and Thresholds

1. **Primary Metrics**
   - NPV10: Must be positive under base case
   - NPV15: Strategic projects should have positive NPV15
   - IRR: Must exceed hurdle rate with 3% minimum buffer
   - PIR (Profit-to-Investment Ratio): Target > 0.3

2. **Secondary Metrics**
   - Payback period: Target < 5 years for conventional, < 3 years for short-cycle
   - Capital efficiency ($/boe): Competitive with basin averages
   - Unit operating cost: Target lower quartile for basin
   - Exposure ratio: Maximum 25% of capital at risk before positive cash flow

3. **Risk Metrics**
   - Value at Risk (95%): Maximum 30% of expected NPV
   - Downside case NPV: Must remain positive under P90 case
   - Break-even price: Target below forward curve
   - Minimum acceptable probability of positive NPV: 80%

## Sensitivity Analysis Requirements

### Standard Sensitivity Variables

1. **Market Factors**
   - Commodity prices: ±15%, ±30%
   - Product differentials: ±50%, ±100% of base assumption
   - Exchange rates: ±10%, ±20%
   - Inflation: ±2%, ±4% absolute

2. **Operational Factors**
   - Capital costs: ±15%, ±30%
   - Operating costs: ±20%, ±40%
   - Production rates: ±10%, ±20%
   - Project schedule: +6 months, +12 months

3. **Fiscal Factors**
   - Tax rates: +5%, +10% absolute
   - Royalty rates: +3%, +6% absolute
   - Carbon pricing: $0, $50, $100 per tonne CO2e

### Multi-Variable Analysis

1. **Required Scenario Sets**
   - Base Case: Corporate approved assumptions
   - Low Case: P90 reserves, -15% price, +15% costs
   - High Case: P10 reserves, +15% price, -10% costs
   - Disaster Case: P90 reserves, -30% price, +30% costs
   - Market Shock: Cyclical price crash followed by recovery
   - Carbon Policy: Base case with elevated carbon price

2. **Monte Carlo Simulation Parameters**
   - Minimum iterations: 10,000
   - Output distributions: NPV, IRR, payback, production
   - Correlation matrix: Standard corporate matrix with adjustments for project specifics
   - Tornado chart: Required for all key variables

## Portfolio Optimization Framework

### Project Ranking Methodology

1. **Economic Efficiency Metrics**
   - NPV per dollar invested
   - IRR vs. hurdle rate premium
   - Discounted Profitability Index (DPI)

2. **Strategic Alignment Factors**
   - Core area growth contribution (0-5 scale)
   - Technology advancement value (0-3 scale)
   - Portfolio diversification benefit (0-3 scale)

3. **Risk Adjustment Process**
   - Certainty equivalent NPV calculation
   - Risk-weighted rate of return
   - Sharpe ratio equivalent for project portfolio

### Optimization Constraints

1. **Capital Allocation Limits**
   - Maximum 25% capital to single project
   - Maximum 40% capital to single basin
   - Minimum 15% capital to low-risk projects
   - Maximum 20% capital to high-risk projects

2. **Corporate Financial Constraints**
   - Debt/EBITDA ratio < 2.5x
   - Interest coverage ratio > 4.0x
   - Minimum free cash flow target of 15% of CFO
