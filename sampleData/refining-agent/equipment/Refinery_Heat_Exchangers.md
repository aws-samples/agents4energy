# Refinery Heat Exchangers: Types, Applications, and Specifications

## Introduction
Heat exchangers are critical equipment in refineries, used to transfer heat between two or more fluids without direct contact. They are essential for energy recovery, product cooling/heating, and process temperature control.

## Types of Heat Exchangers in Refineries

### Shell and Tube Heat Exchangers
The most common type in refineries, comprising 60-70% of all heat exchangers.

**Design Characteristics:**
- Shell: cylindrical vessel containing bundle of tubes
- Tubes: heat transfer surface
- Baffles: direct flow across tube bundle and provide support
- Tube sheets: separate shell and tube side fluids
- Channels: inlet/outlet sections for the tube side fluid

**Common Applications:**
- Crude preheat trains
- Overhead condensers
- Product coolers
- Reboilers
- Interchangers between process streams

**Typical Specifications:**
- Heat transfer area: 10-5,000 m²
- Shell diameter: 0.2-2.5 m
- Tube length: 2-12 m
- Maximum operating pressure: Up to 300 bar
- Temperature range: -100°C to 600°C
- Materials: Carbon steel, stainless steel, duplex, Inconel

### Plate Heat Exchangers

**Design Characteristics:**
- Stack of corrugated metal plates with gaskets
- Higher heat transfer coefficients than shell and tube
- More compact design
- Lower fouling tendency

**Common Applications:**
- Low-fouling services
- Low to medium pressure applications
- Overhead condensers
- Water cooling systems
- Lube oil cooling

**Typical Specifications:**
- Heat transfer area: 1-2,500 m²
- Plate size: 0.1-3.0 m² per plate
- Maximum operating pressure: Up to 25 bar
- Temperature range: -35°C to 200°C
- Materials: Stainless steel, titanium, nickel alloys

### Air Cooled Heat Exchangers (Fin-Fan Coolers)

**Design Characteristics:**
- Finned tubes with forced air cooling
- Multiple tube bundles with fans
- Used when cooling water is limited

**Common Applications:**
- Product cooling
- Overhead condensers
- Compressor interstage cooling
- Gas cooling

**Typical Specifications:**
- Heat transfer area: 50-10,000 m²
- Fan diameter: 1.8-4.0 m
- Air flow rate: 10,000-300,000 m³/h
- Maximum operating pressure: Up to 100 bar
- Temperature range: -40°C to 400°C

## Heat Exchanger Performance

### Key Performance Parameters
- Heat duty (Q): Total heat transferred (kW)
- Overall heat transfer coefficient (U): Heat transfer rate per unit area per degree of temperature difference (W/m²·K)
- Log mean temperature difference (LMTD): Effective temperature driving force (°C)
- Pressure drop (ΔP): Resistance to flow on each side (bar)
- Fouling resistance (Rf): Additional thermal resistance due to deposits (m²·K/W)

### Typical Performance Values
- Shell and tube U values: 100-1,000 W/m²·K
- Plate exchanger U values: 1,000-5,000 W/m²·K
- Air cooler U values: 25-200 W/m²·K
- Acceptable pressure drops: 0.5-1.0 bar (liquids), 0.1-0.3 bar (gases)

## Maintenance Considerations

### Common Problems
- Fouling: Deposition of unwanted materials on heat transfer surfaces
- Corrosion: Chemical attack on materials of construction
- Erosion: Physical damage from high velocity or particulates
- Thermal stress: Expansion/contraction damage from temperature changes

### Monitoring Parameters
- Temperature approach: Difference between outlet and target temperatures
- Pressure drop: Increase indicates fouling
- Heat transfer coefficient: Decrease indicates fouling or other issues

### Cleaning Methods
- Chemical cleaning: Circulation of solvents, acids, or detergents
- Mechanical cleaning: Hydroblasting, bullet cleaning, or manual cleaning
- Online cleaning: Chemical additives during operation

## Energy Efficiency Considerations

### Heat Integration Strategies
- Crude preheat trains: 65-75% of heating duty recovered from products
- Pinch analysis: Systematic approach to maximize heat recovery
- Improved U values: Enhanced surface geometries, tube inserts

### Efficiency Metrics
- Energy Recovery Factor: Ratio of recovered heat to total heat requirement
- Pinch temperature approach: Minimum temperature difference between hot and cold streams
- Overall thermal efficiency: Useful heat transfer divided by total heat input
