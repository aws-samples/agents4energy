# Crude Distillation Process Overview

## Introduction
The Crude Distillation Unit (CDU) is the first processing unit in virtually all petroleum refineries. The CDU distills the incoming crude oil into various fractions of different boiling ranges, each of which are then processed further in the other refinery processing units.

## Process Description

### Feed Preparation
1. Crude oil from storage tanks is pumped through heat exchangers
2. Preheated crude passes through a desalter to remove salts, water and solids
3. Desalted crude is further heated in a series of heat exchangers and a fired heater
4. Heated crude (typically 330-370°C) enters the atmospheric distillation column

### Atmospheric Distillation Column
The atmospheric distillation column operates at a pressure slightly above atmospheric pressure (1.0-1.5 bar) and contains 30-40 trays. The column separates crude oil into the following streams:

- Overhead products: Light gases and naphtha (C1-C12, <180°C)
- Side-draw products:
  - Light naphtha (C5-C7, 30-90°C)
  - Heavy naphtha (C7-C10, 90-160°C)
  - Kerosene (C10-C16, 160-250°C)
  - Light gas oil (C14-C20, 250-300°C)
  - Heavy gas oil (C20-C25, 300-370°C)
- Bottom product: Atmospheric residue (>C25, >370°C)

### Column Operation
1. Preheated crude enters the column at the flash zone
2. Lighter components rise up the column while heavier components flow down
3. Stripping steam injected at the bottom to enhance separation
4. Side-draw products pass through side strippers to remove lighter components
5. Reflux streams maintain temperature profiles for optimal separation

## Process Parameters

### Typical Operating Conditions
- Column feed temperature: 330-370°C
- Column bottom temperature: 350-385°C
- Column top temperature: 110-130°C
- Column top pressure: 1.0-1.5 bar
- Reflux ratio: 0.8-1.2

### Heat Integration
- Feed/product heat exchangers recover heat from hot products to preheat the crude
- Energy efficiency: 85-90% heat recovery is typical in modern refineries

## Equipment
- Desalter: Removes water-soluble salts and solids
- Fired heater: Primary heating source for the crude
- Main fractionation column: 30-40 trays, typically 3-5m diameter and 30-40m height
- Side strippers: Remove light components from side-draw products
- Overhead condenser: Cools overhead vapors
- Product coolers: Reduce product temperatures for storage

## Performance Metrics
- Capacity: 50,000-250,000 barrels per day
- Energy consumption: 0.1-0.14 GJ/barrel of crude
- Separation efficiency: 95-99%
- Desalting efficiency: 85-95% salt removal

## Safety Considerations
- High-temperature operations require careful monitoring
- Hydrocarbon vapors present fire and explosion hazards
- Steam and hot condensate pose burn hazards
- Emergency shutdown systems must be tested regularly

## Environmental Impact
- Furnace flue gas emissions: NOx, CO, CO2, particulates
- Wastewater from desalter contains oil, salts, phenols
- Modern units incorporate vapor recovery systems to minimize VOC emissions
