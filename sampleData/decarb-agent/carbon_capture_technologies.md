# Carbon Capture Technologies in Energy Operations

## Post-Combustion Capture Technologies

### Amine-Based Absorption Systems
1. **Process Description**
   - Flue gas contacts amine solution in absorber column
   - CO₂ chemically binds with amine compounds
   - Rich amine solution regenerated in stripper column using heat
   - Typical amines: MEA (monoethanolamine), DEA, MDEA, piperazine blends
   - Capture rate: 85-95% of CO₂ from flue gas

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 2.5-3.5 GJ/tonne CO₂ captured
   - Amine concentration: 15-40% by weight
   - Absorber temperature: 40-60°C
   - Stripper temperature: 100-140°C
   - Pressure: Near atmospheric in absorber, 1.5-2 bar in stripper

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Ideal for retrofit applications at existing facilities
   - Applicable to power plants, refineries, and industrial facilities
   - Best for flue gas with CO₂ concentrations of 3-15%
   - Tolerance for impurities: Moderate (requires SOx/NOx pre-treatment)
   - Scale: Commercial (up to 1 MtCO₂/year facilities operating)

4. **Advantages and Limitations**
   - Advantages:
     * Proven commercial technology
     * High capture efficiency
     * Adaptable to various flue gas compositions
     * Significant operational experience
   - Limitations:
     * High energy requirements for regeneration
     * Amine degradation issues
     * Corrosion concerns
     * Environmental considerations for amine emissions

### Carbonate-Based Systems
1. **Process Description**
   - Flue gas contacts carbonate solution (typically potassium carbonate)
   - CO₂ reacts to form bicarbonate
   - Thermal regeneration releases captured CO₂
   - Often uses catalysts or promoters to enhance reaction rates
   - Integrated with heat recovery systems

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 2.0-3.0 GJ/tonne CO₂ captured
   - Solution concentration: 15-40% K₂CO₃
   - Operating temperature: 60-80°C
   - Regeneration temperature: 120-150°C
   - Pressure: Atmospheric to 1.5 bar

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Suited for high-pressure applications
   - Applicable to refinery hydrogen plants and natural gas processing
   - Optimal for gas streams with high CO₂ partial pressure
   - Tolerance for sulfur compounds: Higher than amines
   - Scale: Demonstration to early commercial

### Membrane Separation Systems
1. **Process Description**
   - Selective polymeric or inorganic membranes
   - Differential pressure drives CO₂ permeation
   - Multi-stage systems for high capture rates
   - No regeneration energy required but compression needed
   - Can be combined with other capture technologies

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 1.0-1.5 GJ/tonne CO₂ (plus compression)
   - CO₂ permeance: 1000-3000 GPU (gas permeation units)
   - CO₂/N₂ selectivity: 20-80
   - Pressure ratio: 5-15
   - Operating temperature: 0-100°C (polymer dependent)

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Best for medium-to-high CO₂ concentration streams (>10%)
   - Applicable to natural gas processing and biogas upgrading
   - Challenging for dilute flue gas without pre-concentration
   - Space efficiency advantage for offshore or space-constrained facilities
   - Scale: Commercial for high-concentration applications, demonstration for flue gas

## Pre-Combustion Capture Technologies

### Integrated Gasification Combined Cycle (IGCC) with CCS
1. **Process Description**
   - Feedstock gasification produces syngas (CO + H₂)
   - Water-gas shift reaction converts CO to CO₂
   - CO₂ separation prior to combustion
   - H₂-rich gas combusted in gas turbine
   - Capture methods include physical solvents (Selexol, Rectisol)

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 1.0-1.6 GJ/tonne CO₂ captured
   - Operating pressure: 30-80 bar
   - CO₂ concentration after WGS: 35-40%
   - Capture rate: 85-95%
   - Overall efficiency penalty: 7-9 percentage points

3. **Application Suitability**
   - New-build power generation facilities
   - Hydrogen production with carbon capture
   - Refineries for hydrogen production
   - Synthetic fuel production
   - Scale: Large demonstration to early commercial

### Hydrogen Production with CO₂ Capture
1. **Process Description**
   - Steam methane reforming (SMR) or autothermal reforming (ATR)
   - Water-gas shift to convert CO to CO₂ and H₂
   - CO₂ separation using physical or chemical solvents
   - Pressure swing adsorption for H₂ purification
   - CO₂ compression for transport and storage

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 1.4-2.0 GJ/tonne CO₂ captured
   - Operating pressure: 15-40 bar
   - Capture rate: 60-90% for SMR, up to 95% for ATR
   - Hydrogen purity: >99.9%
   - CO₂ output: 9-10 kg CO₂/kg H₂ (without capture)

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Refinery hydrogen production
   - Ammonia synthesis
   - Methanol production
   - Blue hydrogen for energy applications
   - Scale: Commercial

## Oxy-Combustion Technologies

### Oxy-Fuel Combustion
1. **Process Description**
   - Oxygen separation from air using cryogenic or membrane processes
   - Combustion in pure oxygen with recycled flue gas
   - Resulting flue gas high in CO₂ concentration (80-95%)
   - Minimal gas separation required post-combustion
   - Water removal by condensation

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 1.5-2.5 GJ/tonne CO₂ (including ASU)
   - Oxygen purity: 95-99.5%
   - Combustion temperature control: Critical
   - CO₂ purity after processing: >95%
   - Capture rate: >98%

3. **Application Suitability**
   - New-build power plants
   - Industrial boilers and furnaces
   - Cement kilns
   - Glass furnaces
   - Scale: Demonstration

### Chemical Looping Combustion
1. **Process Description**
   - Metal oxide oxygen carrier circulates between reactors
   - Fuel oxidation by metal oxide in absence of air
   - Reduced metal reoxidized in air reactor
   - Inherent CO₂ separation without gas separation equipment
   - Various metal oxides used (iron, nickel, copper, manganese)

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 0.5-1.0 GJ/tonne CO₂
   - Operating temperature: 800-1000°C
   - Pressure: Atmospheric to 10 bar
   - Carrier lifetime: Critical cost factor
   - Capture rate: >95%

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Next-generation power plants
   - Industrial heating applications
   - Hydrogen production
   - Scale: Pilot (MW-scale demonstrations underway)

## Direct Air Capture (DAC) Technologies

### Solid Sorbent DAC Systems
1. **Process Description**
   - Air contacts solid CO₂-selective sorbents
   - Temperature or pressure swing regeneration
   - Modular design with multiple adsorption units
   - Low-temperature heat used for regeneration
   - Continuous or batch operation

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 4.0-8.0 GJ/tonne CO₂
   - Thermal energy: 80-120°C for regeneration
   - Sorbent capacity: 1-3 mol CO₂/kg sorbent
   - Sorbent lifetime: Critical cost driver
   - CO₂ purity: >99% after processing

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Distributed carbon capture
   - Integration with renewable energy
   - Negative emissions technology
   - Co-located with CO₂ utilization
   - Scale: Early commercial (kilotonne scale)

### Liquid Solvent DAC Systems
1. **Process Description**
   - Air contacts alkaline hydroxide solution
   - CO₂ forms carbonate/bicarbonate
   - Regeneration through causticization or calcination
   - High-temperature heat required for regeneration
   - Continuous operation with multiple process steps

2. **Technical Specifications**
   - Energy requirement: 7.0-12.0 GJ/tonne CO₂
   - Thermal energy: >800°C for calcination
   - Electrical energy: 1.5-2.5 GJ/tonne CO₂
   - Solution concentration: 1-3 M hydroxide
   - CO₂ purity: >99% after processing

3. **Application Suitability**
   - Centralized large-scale carbon removal
   - Integration with industrial waste heat
   - Carbon-neutral fuel production
   - Scale: Early commercial (kilotonne scale)

## CO₂ Conversion and Utilization

### CO₂ to Fuels and Chemicals
1. **Catalytic Hydrogenation to Methanol**
   - CO₂ + 3H₂ → CH₃OH + H₂O
   - Copper-based catalysts typical
   - Operating conditions: 200-300°C, 50-100 bar
   - Energy requirement: Highly dependent on hydrogen source
   - Commercial status: Demonstration plants operating

2. **Electrochemical Reduction**
   - Direct electron transfer to CO₂
   - Products include CO, formic acid, ethylene, ethanol
   - Catalyst and electrolyte determine selectivity
   - Energy requirement: 10-30 kWh/kg product
   - Commercial status: Early pilot stage

3. **Thermochemical Pathways**
   - Dry reforming of methane (CO₂ + CH₄ → 2CO + 2H₂)
   - Reverse water-gas shift (CO₂ + H₂ → CO + H₂O)
   - Fischer-Tropsch synthesis for liquid hydrocarbons
   - Energy intensive but established processes
   - Commercial status: Varies by process (pilot to commercial)

### CO₂ Mineralization
1. **Ex-situ Mineralization**
   - Reaction with calcium or magnesium-rich materials
   - Products include carbonates (CaCO₃, MgCO₃)
   - Permanent CO₂ storage in solid form
   - Potential use in construction materials
   - Commercial status: Early commercial

2. **In-situ Mineralization**
   - Injection of CO₂ into suitable geological formations
   - Natural reaction with basaltic or peridotite rocks
   - Rapid mineralization (years to decades)
   - Permanent and secure CO₂ storage
   - Commercial status: Demonstration projects
