# New Mexico Drilling Permit Requirements Guide

## Overview of New Mexico Oil & Gas Regulatory Framework

In New Mexico, oil and gas operations are regulated primarily by the **Oil Conservation Division (OCD)** of the Energy, Minerals and Natural Resources Department. The OCD has jurisdiction over:

- Well drilling, completion, and plugging operations
- Well spacing and location requirements
- Production reporting and allocation
- Underground injection for disposal or enhanced recovery
- Environmental compliance related to oil and gas activities

Different agencies may have overlapping jurisdiction depending on:
- **Surface ownership** (private, state, federal)
- **Mineral ownership** (private, state, federal)
- **Geographic location** (including tribal lands)

## Pre-Drilling Requirements Checklist

### 1. Required OCD Forms

| Form Number | Form Name | Purpose | When Required | Filing Timeline |
|-------------|-----------|---------|---------------|----------------|
| **C-101** | Application for Permit to Drill (APD) | Request permission to drill a new well | All new wells | At least 10 days before spudding |
| **C-102** | Well Location and Acreage Dedication Plat | Certify well location and dedicated acreage | All new wells | With C-101 |
| **C-103** | Sundry Notices and Reports on Wells | Request approval for various well activities | Various operations | Varies by activity |
| **C-105** | Well Completion or Recompletion Report | Report well completion details | All completed wells | Within 20 days of completion |
| **C-111** | Application for Authorization to Inject | Request permission for injection | Injection wells | Before injection operations |
| **C-108** | Application to Dispose of Salt Water by Injection | Request disposal well approval | Disposal wells | Before disposal operations |

### 2. Surface Owner and Access Requirements

For **Private Surface**:
- Surface Use Agreement or proof of surface owner notification
- Road access agreements if crossing other properties

For **State Surface**:
- Surface Use Agreement from New Mexico State Land Office
- Additional reclamation bond may be required

For **Federal Surface**:
- BLM Right-of-Way for access roads if needed
- Surface Use Plan approved by BLM

### 3. Bonding Requirements

| Bond Type | Amount | Required For | Authority |
|-----------|--------|--------------|-----------|
| Single Well Financial Assurance | $25,000 | Individual well | OCD |
| Blanket Financial Assurance | $50,000 | Multiple wells (up to 10) | OCD |
| Blanket Financial Assurance | $250,000 | Over 10 wells | OCD |
| BLM Bond | $10,000 (single well) | Wells on federal land | BLM |
| BLM Statewide Bond | $25,000 | Multiple wells on federal land in NM | BLM |
| BLM Nationwide Bond | $150,000 | Wells on federal land nationwide | BLM |

### 4. Spacing and Location Requirements

**Standard Spacing** (unless modified by special pool orders):

| Well Type | Ownership Pattern | Spacing Unit Size | Minimum Distance Requirements |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------------------|
| Oil Well | Conventional | 40 acres | 330' from property/unit lines |
| Gas Well | Conventional | 160 acres | 660' from property/unit lines |
| Horizontal Oil Well | Standard | 640 acres (or established spacing) | Varies by basin (see below) |

**Horizontal Well Setback Requirements by Basin**:

- **San Juan Basin**:
  - 330' from outer boundaries (oil wells)
  - 660' from outer boundaries (gas wells)
  
- **Permian Basin**:
  - 330' from outer boundaries (oil wells)
  - 660' from outer boundaries (gas wells)
  - Special rules for Bone Spring, Wolfcamp, and other formations

### 5. Special Requirements by County

| County | Additional Requirements | Local Authority | Timeline |
|--------|-------------------------|-----------------|----------|
| **Eddy** | Water use reporting | Eddy County | With APD |
| **Lea** | Special road use permits for heavy vehicles | Lea County | 15 days before use |
| **San Juan** | Air quality notification | San Juan County | 30 days before spud |
| **Rio Arriba** | Additional cultural resource survey | Rio Arriba County | 45 days before spud |
| **Sandoval** | Additional wildlife survey | Sandoval County | 45 days before spud |

## Drilling Permit (C-101) Application Components

A complete C-101 Application for Permit to Drill must include:

1. **Basic Well Information**
   - Operator information (name, OGRID number, address)
   - Proposed well name and number
   - Well type (vertical, directional, horizontal)
   - Target formation(s)
   - Surface and bottomhole locations

2. **Technical Information**
   - Proposed total depth
   - Estimated tops of important formations
   - Casing program details
   - Cementing program details
   - Expected H₂S concentration

3. **Attachments**
   - Form C-102 (Well Location Plat)
   - Well bore schematic/diagram
   - Closed-loop drilling system details
   - H₂S contingency plan (if applicable)
   - Water use and management plan

4. **Special Requirements for Horizontal Wells**
   - Proposed lateral length
   - Proposed azimuth
   - Vertical section plane details
   - Cross-section showing all target formations
   - Distance to other laterals in same target

## Environmental Considerations

### Water Protection Requirements

1. **Surface Casing**:
   - Must be set below all fresh water zones
   - Typically 10% of planned total depth or 1,000', whichever is greater
   - Must be cemented to surface

2. **Pit/Closed-Loop Requirements**:
   - In many areas, closed-loop systems are required
   - Temporary pits require specific liner specifications
   - Must provide details on pit construction or closed-loop system

3. **Groundwater Monitoring**:
   - May be required in sensitive areas
   - Baseline testing often recommended

### Cultural and Wildlife Resources

1. **Cultural Resource Survey**:
   - Required for all federal and state lands
   - May be required on private lands in certain counties

2. **Wildlife Protection**:
   - Timing limitations in certain habitats
   - Special procedures for protected species areas

## Common Pitfalls and Solutions

| Common Issue | Potential Consequence | Solution |
|--------------|------------------------|----------|
| Inadequate surface owner notification | Permit denial or delay | Document all notification attempts; send via certified mail |
| Inaccurate well plats | Permit rejection | Have professional surveyor prepare plats |
| Missing water protection plan | Permit denial | Include detailed fresh water protection measures |
| Incomplete casing/cementing program | Request for additional information | Follow OCD guidelines for minimum requirements |
| Improper spacing | Hearing required | Apply for spacing exception if needed |

## Recent Regulatory Changes (As of 2023)

1. **Methane Waste Rule** (effective May 2021)
   - Requires capture of 98% of methane by 2026
   - Impacts equipment selection and operation

2. **Enhanced Reporting Requirements** (effective January 2022)
   - More detailed spill notification process
   - Additional water use reporting

3. **Financial Assurance Increases** (effective July 2022)
   - Increased bonding amounts
   - New requirements for inactive wells

4. **Horizontal Well Spacing Revisions** (effective March 2023)
   - Updated spacing requirements for Bone Spring and Wolfcamp formations
   - New notification requirements for adjacent operators
